Step into a beautifully rendered sacred space where every detail — from the warm glow of oil lamps to the soft drift of incense smoke — is designed to evoke the feeling of standing before a living deity. Divine Presence recreates the atmosphere of a traditional Hindu temple, inviting you to slow down, be present, and offer your devotion in a spiritually immersive 3D environment.

## The Temple Environment

The temple is modeled after a classical South Indian architectural style, featuring towering gopurams at the entrance, stone-carved pillars, and a sanctified inner chamber housing the deity idol. Flower petals are scattered across the stone floor, incense smoke curls gently upward from brass holders, and devotional music plays softly in the background — all coming together to create a space that feels both sacred and alive.

<Note>
  The temple lighting changes subtly based on the real-world time of day. Morning sessions feature soft golden light filtering through latticed windows, while evening sessions are lit by warm lamp-glow and a deep indigo sky visible through the outer courtyard.
</Note>

The temple is divided into four distinct zones, each serving a different purpose in your worship journey. Move freely between them or follow the natural path from entrance to sanctum.

## Performance Tips

Divine Presence is optimized for a wide range of devices, but the rich visual effects — volumetric incense smoke, dynamic lighting, and particle-based flower petals — can be demanding on older hardware.

If you notice slowdowns or frame drops, navigate to **Settings → Graphics** and consider the following adjustments:

- **Disable Volumetric Smoke** — reduces GPU load significantly while keeping incense visible as a simpler effect
- **Lower Shadow Quality** — set to Medium or Low for a smoother experience on older devices
- **Reduce Particle Density** — lowers the number of flower petals rendered on the floor
- **Turn off Ambient Occlusion** — has minimal visual impact but noticeably improves performance

These settings are saved per device, so you can maintain a high-quality experience on your primary device while using a lighter configuration on others.
