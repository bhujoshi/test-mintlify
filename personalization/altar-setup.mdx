Your personal altar is your sacred space within the temple — a platform you control entirely. Arrange deity idols, decorative items, flowers, lamps, and cherished personal objects to build a shrine that feels genuinely yours. Every arrangement you make persists across sessions, so the moment you step into the temple, your altar greets you exactly as you left it.

## Opening the Altar Editor

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Your Altar">
    From the main temple hall, approach your personal altar platform. When the **Edit Altar** prompt appears at the bottom of the screen, press or tap it to enter the Altar Editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an Altar Cloth">
    Select the **Cloth** tab in the editor panel. Pick from 12 colors and patterns — including plain cotton, silk weaves, and gold-embroidered designs. Tap any swatch to preview it live on your altar before confirming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place Your Deity Idols">
    Open the **Idols** tab and drag up to three deity idols onto the altar platform. Drop each one onto a glowing placement node. Pinch to resize or rotate an idol so it sits at exactly the angle you prefer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Decorative Items">
    Switch to the **Décor** tab and choose from the available sacred objects. Drag items onto the altar surface or onto any open shelf space. Reposition them freely until the arrangement feels right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a Photo Frame">
    In the **Photos** tab, tap **Upload Photo** and select an image from your device — a family deity photo or an heirloom picture works perfectly. The editor fits your image into an ornate digital photo frame placed on the altar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the Flower Garland">
    Open the **Flowers** tab and choose a pre-arranged garland style. The garland drapes around the idol automatically; you can adjust drape length and flower type from the dropdown menus.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Your Altar">
    Tap **Save Altar** in the top-right corner of the editor. Your layout is committed to your account and will appear on every future visit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Altar Item Categories

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Cloths & Coverings" icon="shirt">
    Choose from 12 altar cloth options — plain cotton, draped silk, and gold-embroidered ceremonial fabrics. Each cloth sets the visual foundation for your entire altar arrangement.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Deity Idols" icon="hands-praying">
    Place up to three deity idols on designated nodes. Each idol is a detailed 3D model rendered with traditional iconography, and placement is fully drag-and-drop.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sacred Décor Objects" icon="star-of-life">
    Adorn your altar with traditional items: diyas (oil lamps), a conch shell, a brass bell, a kalash (ceremonial pot), a coconut, and fresh banana leaves.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flower Garlands" icon="leaf">
    Drape pre-arranged garlands of marigold, rose, or jasmine around your idol. Garland style and length are adjustable so the flowers complement your idol's proportions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Photo Frames" icon="image">
    Upload a personal photo — a family deity picture, a guru portrait, or a cherished temple photograph — and display it in a decorative frame on your altar.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Lamps & Flames" icon="fire">
    Position diyas and ghee lamps with animated flickering flames. Adjust the number of flames and their placement to match your puja ritual preferences.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Free vs. Premium Item Catalog

The free tier gives you access to **15 altar items** covering the essentials: one cloth, two idol slots, a basic diya, and a garland. Upgrading to a premium subscription unlocks the full catalog — all 12 cloth designs, the third idol slot, every decorative object, the photo frame feature, and all garland varieties.

<Note>
  Altar customizations are saved directly to your account. Every device you sign in on will show your altar exactly as you arranged it — no need to set it up again.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Enable **Festive Mode** in the Altar Editor before an upcoming Hindu festival and your altar will be automatically decorated with festival-appropriate items — for example, a rangoli base cloth and marigold garlands for Diwali, or a peacock feather arrangement for Janmashtami. Festive Mode activates the night before each festival and reverts to your saved layout the day after.
</Tip>
