Divine Presence lets you tune every aspect of the temple atmosphere — from the swell of devotional music to the flicker of a ghee lamp to the smoothness of incense smoke rising in the air. Whether you prefer a quiet pre-dawn ritual or a fully immersive evening aarti, Ambient Settings put every sensory detail under your control. Settings are saved per-device, so your home tablet can carry different preferences than your phone.

## Adjusting Your Settings

Open Ambient Settings by tapping the **Settings** icon (the gear wheel) in the top-right corner of the temple screen. The panel is divided into four tabs: Audio, Lighting, Graphics, and Notifications.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Audio">
    Control every layer of sound within the temple so the audio environment matches the mood and depth of your practice.

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Devotional Music Volume** | Adjusts the volume of background bhajans, kirtans, and classical compositions playing in the temple. Set to 0 to worship in silence. |
    | **Mantra Audio Volume** | Controls the chanting tracks that play during specific offering rituals (for example, a Ganesha mantra during modak offerings). Independent of music volume. |
    | **Ambient Sound Effects** | Blends environmental sounds into the experience — temple bells, birdsong, and a gentle river. Use the slider to set how prominently these layer over the music. |
    | **Bell Sounds** | Toggles the resonant bell chime that plays when you ring the temple bell during puja. |
    | **Birdsong** | Adds a soft outdoor ambience of birds, suitable for morning or afternoon sessions. |
    | **River Sounds** | Introduces the calming sound of flowing water — ideal for meditative or early-morning worship. |
    | **Narration Language** | Sets the language for ritual guidance and on-screen narration. Choose from **English, Hindi, Tamil, Telugu,** or **Kannada**. |

    <Tip>
      Activating **Focus Mode** from the Quick Presets menu automatically reduces Audio to mantra-only playback, mutes ambient effects, and dims all lighting except the area around the deity idol — perfect for deep meditation or personal prayer.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Lighting">
    Shape the visual mood of your temple by controlling the time of day, lamp brightness, and flame behavior.

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Time of Day** | Shifts the ambient temple lighting through four presets — **Morning** (soft golden sunrise hues), **Afternoon** (bright, clear daylight), **Evening** (warm amber and orange), and **Night** (deep indigo with lamplight prominence). |
    | **Lamp Brightness** | Raises or lowers the intensity of all diyas and lamps on your altar and in the main hall. Increase brightness for a celebratory atmosphere or reduce it for contemplative practice. |
    | **Candle Flicker Intensity** | Controls how vigorously lamp flames animate. Set to **Low** for a steady, meditative flame, **Medium** for a realistic gentle flicker, or **High** for a dynamic, wind-touched effect. |
    | **Ambient Light Color Temperature** | Shifts the overall room light between warm (golden) and cool (moonlit white) tones to complement your chosen time of day. |
    | **Shadow Depth** | Sets how defined the shadows cast by idols and décor objects appear. Higher depth increases realism; lower depth softens the scene. |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Graphics">
    Match visual fidelity to your device's capability so the temple always runs smoothly without sacrificing the experience.

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Quality Preset** | Applies a global rendering preset: **Low** (optimized for older or budget devices), **Medium** (balanced performance and detail), **High** (full textures and reflections), or **Ultra** (maximum detail for high-end hardware). |
    | **Particle Effects** | Toggles animated particles throughout the temple — incense smoke curling upward, flower petals drifting from garlands, and water droplets during abhishek. Disable to improve performance on low-end devices. |
    | **Incense Smoke** | Specifically controls the volumetric smoke simulation from burning dhoop and agarbatti. Can be disabled independently of other particle effects. |
    | **Flower Petals** | Toggles the petal-drift effect that plays during flower-offering rituals. |
    | **Shadow Quality** | Fine-tunes shadow resolution: **Off**, **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**. Shadows at High add significant visual depth but require more processing power. |
    | **Anti-Aliasing** | Smooths jagged edges on curved idol surfaces and architecture. Options are **Off**, **FXAA** (fast, slight softening), and **MSAA** (sharper, higher cost). |
    | **Frame Rate Cap** | Limits the maximum frame rate to **30 fps** or **60 fps**, helping manage battery usage during longer sessions. |

    <Note>
      If you notice slowdowns or overheating, set Quality Preset to **Low** and turn off Particle Effects. These two changes alone recover the most performance and are recommended for entry-level smartphones and tablets.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Notifications">
    Let Divine Presence keep your practice consistent with timely, respectful reminders — never intrusive, always optional.

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Daily Worship Reminder** | Set a specific time each day to receive a gentle push notification inviting you to begin your puja. Tap the time field to choose your preferred hour and minute. |
    | **Reminder Sound** | Chooses the notification tone: a soft bell chime, a conch call, or your device's default notification sound. |
    | **Festival Alerts** | Sends a notification the day before major Hindu festivals (Diwali, Navratri, Ganesh Chaturthi, Janmashtami, and others) reminding you to prepare your altar and activating Festive Mode. |
    | **Streak Reminders** | Notifies you if you are close to midnight without completing your daily worship streak, helping you maintain your consecutive-day count. |
    | **Streak Reminder Lead Time** | Sets how many hours before midnight the streak reminder fires — choose **1 hour**, **2 hours**, or **3 hours**. |
    | **Do Not Disturb Hours** | Define a quiet window during which no notifications will be sent, regardless of other settings. Useful if your reminder time and bedtime are close together. |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Quick Presets

Beyond per-setting adjustments, the **Quick Presets** menu (accessible from the top of the Settings panel) gives you one-tap configurations for common worship contexts:

- **Morning Puja** — Warm morning light, birdsong on low, devotional music at medium, High graphics
- **Evening Aarti** — Evening lighting, bell sounds on, music at high volume, lamp brightness maximum
- **Meditation** — Night lighting, river sounds only, music and narration off, all particles disabled
- **Focus Mode** — Idol spotlight only, mantra audio on, all other audio and visual distractions minimized

<Tip>
  **Focus Mode** is the fastest way to create a distraction-free environment. It dims the entire temple scene except for a soft spotlight on your home deity, mutes all audio except your chosen mantra track, and pauses particle animations. Tap **Focus Mode** again to restore your previous settings instantly.
</Tip>
