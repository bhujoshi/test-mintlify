Prayer and mantra chanting form the living heartbeat of Hindu worship. Divine Presence gives you a complete mantra library with high-quality audio playback, on-screen Sanskrit text paired with transliteration and English meaning, and a **digital japa mala** (rosary bead counter) so you can track every repetition of a sacred name. Whether you chant aloud, follow along in silence, or type a personal prayer straight to your deity, the temple is always ready to receive your words.

## Starting a Guided Mantra Session

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Mantras Panel">
    Tap the **Om (ॐ) icon** in the bottom navigation bar to open the Mantras Panel. Your currently active deity is selected by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse the Mantra Library">
    Scroll through the mantra list for your deity. Each entry shows the mantra name, the Sanskrit text, and a short description of its purpose and benefit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Mantra">
    Tap the mantra you wish to chant. The full mantra card expands to show Sanskrit script, Roman transliteration, and an English meaning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Repetition Count">
    Tap the **Repetitions** field and enter the number of times you intend to chant. The traditional count is **108** — a number held sacred across Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain traditions. You can also choose 27, 54, or enter any custom number.
  </Step>
  <Step title='Tap "Begin Chanting"'>
    Tap **Begin Chanting**. The audio playback starts, the japa mala counter activates, and the mantra text scrolls in sync with the recording — Sanskrit on the first line, transliteration beneath it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Count Each Repetition">
    Tap a **mala bead** on the digital rosary displayed at the bottom of the screen after each repetition. The counter advances and the bead glows. Alternatively, enable **Auto-Count with Microphone** (Premium) to let the app detect each chant automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Enable **Mantra Mode** by tapping the crescent-moon icon in the top-right corner of the Mantras Panel. Mantra Mode dims the rest of the temple environment, centers your deity softly in a halo of light, and silences all ambient sounds except the mantra audio — creating an immersive space for focused meditation and japa.
</Tip>

Expand each deity below to see their primary mantra, its meaning, and the traditional intention behind its chanting.

  <Accordion title="🐘 Ganesha — Remover of Obstacles">

    **Meaning:** "Salutations to Ganapati — I bow to the lord of all beginnings."

    Chant this mantra before starting any new endeavour — a project, a journey, a conversation — to invoke Ganesha's blessing and clear the path ahead. It is also chanted at the opening of every puja to invite auspiciousness into the worship space.

    **Mantra:** *Om Shreem Mahalakshmiyei Namaha*

    **Transliteration:** Om Shreem Ma-ha-lak-shmi-yei Na-ma-ha

    **Meaning:** "Salutations to the great Lakshmi — I bow to her who bestows wealth, grace, and auspiciousness."

    The seed syllable *Shreem* carries the vibrational frequency of abundance and beauty. Chanting this mantra cultivates gratitude for material and spiritual blessings, and opens the heart to receive grace.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="🔱 Shiva — The Auspicious One">
    **Mantra:** *Om Namah Shivaya*

    **Transliteration:** Om Na-mah Shi-va-ya

    **Meaning:** "I bow to Shiva — the inner self, the eternal consciousness."

    Among the most widely chanted mantras in the world, the Panchakshara (five-syllable) mantra *Na-mah-shi-va-ya* represents the five elements and Shiva's mastery over creation, preservation, destruction, concealment, and grace.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="🦚 Krishna — The All-Attractive">

    **Meaning:** A direct address to the divine energy (Hare) and the two principal names of the supreme — Krishna (the all-attractive) and Rama (the reservoir of pleasure).

    The Mahamantra is traditionally chanted for 108 complete rounds on the japa mala. It is said to purify the mind and awaken pure devotional love (*bhakti*).

    The seed syllable *Dum* is the bija (seed sound) of Durga's protective power. Chanting this mantra invokes her fierce compassion — the strength to face and overcome challenges, fear, and inner obstacles.
  </Accordion>

## The Digital Japa Mala

The japa mala (rosary) in Divine Presence mirrors a traditional 108-bead mala so that counting feels natural and unhurried.

- **Open the mala** by tapping the bead counter icon (🪬) at the top of the Mantras Panel.
- **Set your count** — choose 27, 54, or 108 repetitions, or enter a custom number.
- **Count manually** by tapping each bead on the screen after every chant. The bead fills with golden light and the counter increments.
- **Use Auto-Count (Premium)** — enable your device microphone and let Divine Presence detect each verbal chant automatically. The bead advances each time your voice completes a repetition.
- **Review your session** — when you reach your target count, a soft chime plays and your session summary shows total time, repetitions completed, and your all-time count for that mantra.

<Note>
  You can offer a **Personal Prayer** to your deity at any time — in your own words, in any language. Tap the **Personal Prayer** button (speech bubble icon) in the Mantras Panel, type your prayer or intention in the text field, and tap **Offer Prayer**. Your prayer is sent as a private, secure offering. A soft light effect on the deity acknowledges your words.
</Note>
