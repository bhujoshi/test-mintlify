Abhishek — the ritual bathing of a deity's idol — is one of the most intimate and powerful acts of devotion in Hindu worship. In Divine Presence, you pour sacred liquids over a beautifully rendered 3D idol and watch them flow in real time, catching the light as they cascade across the deity's form. Whether you offer simple water or the premium five-nectar Panchamrit, each abhishek is an act of loving surrender that draws you closer to the divine.

## How to Perform Abhishek

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Offerings Panel">
    Tap the **lotus icon** in the bottom navigation bar to open the Offerings Panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Abhishek Tab">
    Tap the **Abhishek** tab inside the Offerings Panel. A selection of sacred liquids appears in ornate vessel icons.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Your Liquid">
    Tap the vessel that holds the liquid you wish to offer — water, milk, rose water, honey, or panchamrit. A short description of the liquid's symbolism appears below the selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title='Tap "Pour"'>
    Tap the **Pour** button. Your avatar raises the vessel above the idol with both hands.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the 3D Flow">
    A shimmering stream of liquid flows over the idol in real time. The fluid catches the temple light, shimmers down the deity's form, and pools gently at the base — a meditative, visually stunning moment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sacred Liquids and Their Meanings

Each liquid offered in abhishek carries a distinct spiritual significance. Select the tab below to explore what each offering represents and which deities receive it with special grace.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Water">
    **Symbolism:** Purity and cleansing — water washes away impurity and renews the spirit.

    Offering water is the most universal form of abhishek and is appropriate for every deity in Divine Presence. It represents your sincere intention to approach the divine with a clean heart, unburdened by worldly distraction.

    **Especially appreciated by:** All deities — water is universally accepted and forms the foundation of every abhishek ritual.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Milk">
    **Symbolism:** Nourishment, purity, and motherly love — milk sustains life and represents selfless giving.

    Milk abhishek connects the devotee to the nurturing aspect of the divine. Its white colour signifies purity of thought and the sattvic (pure, harmonious) quality of your devotion.

    **Especially appreciated by:** **Shiva** (milk is among his most beloved offerings), **Krishna**, and **Lakshmi**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Rose Water">
    **Symbolism:** Fragrance, beauty, and love — rose water carries the essence of devotion itself.

    Pouring rose water over the idol fills the virtual shrine with a sense of sweetness and tenderness. It is associated with the heart's longing for the divine and the beauty of creation.

    **Especially appreciated by:** **Lakshmi**, **Saraswati**, and **Krishna**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Honey">
    **Symbolism:** Sweetness, bliss, and the fruit of patient effort — honey represents the rewards of devoted practice.

    Honey is traditionally offered to invite the sweet grace of the deity into your life. Its golden hue and slow, rich flow create a distinctively lush animation in Divine Presence.

    **Especially appreciated by:** **Ganesha** and **Durga**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Panchamrit ✨">
    **Symbolism:** The five nectars — panchamrit combines milk, curd, honey, ghee, and sugar to represent the wholeness of creation offered back to its source.

    Panchamrit is the most complete and sacred abhishek offering. In Divine Presence, the **Premium Panchamrit** ritual pours all five nectars in sequence, each with its own flowing animation, culminating in a **special divine glow** that illuminates the idol for the rest of your session.

    **Especially appreciated by:** All deities — panchamrit is considered the highest form of liquid offering.

    <Note>**Premium Feature:** The full Panchamrit sequence is available to Premium subscribers. See the note below for details.</Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  The **full Panchamrit Abhishek** — a five-step sequence pouring milk, curd, honey, ghee, and sugar in succession — is a **Premium ritual**. Completing the full sequence unlocks a radiant **divine glow effect** on the idol that persists for the entire session. Free users can still perform a single-liquid water or milk abhishek at any time.
