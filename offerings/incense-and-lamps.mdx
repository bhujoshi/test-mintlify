Lighting incense and performing aarti are among the most sensory-rich moments of puja. The rising smoke of an agarbatti (incense stick) carries your prayers upward; the waving of a diya or lamp during aarti floods the deity with light as an act of reverent offering. In Divine Presence, both rituals are rendered with full 3D fire, particle-based smoke, and dynamic lighting — so the atmosphere of your virtual temple shifts and glows just as it would in a physical shrine.

## Lighting Incense

Incense purifies the space around the deity and creates an atmosphere of sacred focus. Choose from five traditional fragrances, each with its own particle effect and symbolic meaning.

### How to Light Incense

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Offerings Panel">
    Tap the **lotus icon** in the bottom navigation bar to open the Offerings Panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Incense Tab">
    Tap the **Incense** tab. Five agarbatti varieties appear in a fragrance tray.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Your Fragrance">
    Tap the incense stick whose fragrance you wish to offer. A short description of the fragrance and its traditional use appears beneath your selection.

    | Fragrance | Mood & Symbolism |
    |-----------|-----------------|
    | **Sandalwood** | Calm, clarity, and surrender — the classic choice for deep meditation |
    | **Jasmine** | Devotion, purity, and sweetness — particularly beloved for evening puja |
    | **Rose** | Love, grace, and heart-opening — ideal for offerings to Lakshmi and Krishna |
    | **Camphor** | Purification and the dissolution of ego — traditional for Shiva puja |
    | **Frankincense** | Elevation and reverence — associated with high ceremonial worship |
  </Step>
  <Step title='Tap "Light"'>
    Tap the **Light** button. Your avatar touches a flame to the tip of the incense stick.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the Animated Smoke">
    A slender plume of 3D smoke rises from the glowing tip, drifting and curling in a gentle air current. The smoke disperses softly through the temple space, creating an ambient haze around the shrine.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If the smoke effect feels too dense or too faint for your device, navigate to **Settings → Graphics → Smoke Intensity** and adjust the slider. You can also increase or decrease the **Lamp Brightness** for the aarti flame from the same menu.
</Tip>

## Performing Aarti

Aarti is the act of waving a lit lamp before the deity in a circular motion, offering light as the highest visible symbol of devotion. In Divine Presence, you physically move the lamp using your mouse or finger, feeling the rhythm of the ritual in your own hands.

### How to Perform Aarti

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Offerings Panel">
    Tap the **lotus icon** in the bottom navigation bar to open the Offerings Panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Aarti Tab">
    Tap the **Aarti** tab. A decorated brass diya (lamp) rests on the offering tray.
  </Step>
  <Step title='Tap "Begin Aarti"'>
    Tap **Begin Aarti**. The temple lighting dims slightly and the lamp appears in the centre of your screen, flame flickering in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wave the Lamp in a Circular Motion">
    **On desktop:** Click and drag the lamp in slow clockwise circles before the deity.

    **On mobile/tablet:** Press and swipe your finger in clockwise circles across the screen.

    The flame responds to your movement — faster circles create a longer flame trail; slow, deliberate circles produce a steady, meditative glow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete Three Full Rotations">
    Make at least **three complete clockwise rotations** to complete a traditional aarti. The progress ring around the lamp fills as you rotate — a gentle bell tone chimes on each completed circle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Completing a **full aarti** — three complete clockwise rotations — unlocks the **"Blessed Light"** visual effect: a warm golden radiance blooms outward from the deity and lingers over the shrine for the remainder of your session, symbolising the deity's acceptance of your offering.
</Note>

### Aarti Music and Audio Sync

If you have **Devotional Music** enabled in Settings, aarti automatically syncs with the playing audio track. The lamp animation tempo aligns with the rhythm of the aarti song, and the bell tones on each rotation blend naturally into the musical backdrop. To enable devotional music, go to **Settings → Audio → Devotional Music** and toggle it on.

## Combining Incense and Aarti

For the most complete puja atmosphere, light your incense first and then begin aarti — the smoke will still be rising as the lamp flame illuminates the shrine. Both effects render simultaneously, creating a layered, atmospheric scene of smoke, fire, and golden light that brings your virtual temple to life.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I offer incense and aarti to every deity?">
    Yes. Both incense and aarti are universal offerings in Divine Presence and can be performed before any deity in the temple. Certain fragrances have a special affinity for specific deities (for example, camphor for Shiva), but all fragrances are accepted by all deities.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I break the circular motion during aarti?">
    If you pause or break the motion, the progress ring pauses but does not reset. Resume your clockwise movement at any time to continue from where you left off. Only closing the Offerings Panel will reset the aarti session.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do incense and aarti count toward my devotion streak?">
    Yes. Each incense lighting and each completed aarti (three rotations) count as separate devotion actions. Completing both on the same day earns you the **Sacred Fire** daily bonus, which accelerates your streak multiplier.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
